# Visual Direction

## Design Goal

The portfolio should feel like a mature, modern product-engineering portfolio.

It should be clean, calm, readable, and recruiter-friendly while still feeling current and technically capable.

The design should support this positioning:

> Full-stack software developer building SaaS products and practical AI-assisted workflows.

The portfolio should not feel flashy, overly animated, decorative, experimental for its own sake, or like a generic AI-generated portfolio.

## Desired Feeling

The site should feel:

* mature
* calm
* precise
* trustworthy
* modern
* readable
* structured
* product-minded
* quietly technical
* premium but not fancy
* personal but not overly playful

The design should feel closer to a modern SaaS/product website than to a creative agency portfolio.

Useful reference moods:

* Linear
* Vercel
* Stripe Docs
* Notion
* Raycast
* clean developer documentation
* modern B2B SaaS landing pages

These are mood references only. Do not copy any specific brand.

## Visual Principles

### 1. Clarity first

Every section should help the visitor understand Igal’s value faster.

Avoid decorative sections that do not improve understanding.

### 2. Recruiter scanning

Most visitors will scan. Use strong hierarchy, short paragraphs, clear cards, role tags, and obvious calls to action.

### 3. Proof over personality branding

The design should highlight projects, experience, outcomes, technologies, and role fit.

### 4. Calm intelligence

AI should be represented through clarity, structure, and useful interaction, not neon visuals or sci-fi effects.

### 5. Minimal motion

Motion should be subtle and functional. Avoid dramatic page entrances, heavy parallax, constant movement, or flashy gradients.

## Color Direction

Use a neutral, professional base with restrained accents.

Recommended palette direction:

```text
Background: warm off-white / near-white
Primary text: deep charcoal
Secondary text: muted gray
Borders: soft gray
Cards: white or very subtle tinted surfaces
Primary accent: muted indigo or blue
Secondary accent: subtle emerald for AI/automation signals
```

Suggested values:

```text
Background: #FAFAF8 or #F8FAFC
Surface: #FFFFFF
Surface muted: #F3F4F6 or #F1F5F9
Primary text: #111827
Secondary text: #374151
Muted text: #6B7280
Border: #E5E7EB
Primary accent: #4F46E5 or #2563EB
Secondary accent: #10B981
Warning/attention accent: use rarely, e.g. #F59E0B
```

Avoid:

* loud gradients
* neon colors
* heavy purple/coral combinations
* too many accent colors
* high-saturation backgrounds
* dark-mode-first cyber aesthetic

Dark mode can exist, but the main visual identity should work beautifully in light mode.

## Typography

Use clean, readable typography.

Recommended:

* Main font: Inter, Geist, IBM Plex Sans, Manrope, or similar
* Mono font: JetBrains Mono, Geist Mono, Roboto Mono, or similar

Avoid decorative heading fonts.

Typography should communicate:

> I care about clarity, structure, and usability.

Recommended style:

* large but not dramatic hero heading
* strong section headings
* readable body text
* concise technical labels
* generous line height
* consistent spacing

## Layout Direction

Use a structured grid and generous whitespace.

The homepage should feel like a guided product page.

Suggested homepage flow:

1. Hero / positioning
2. Recruiter snapshot
3. Ask My Portfolio AI guide preview
4. Selected work / case studies
5. How I work
6. AI and automation focus
7. Experience timeline
8. Contact CTA

## Hero Section

The hero should be direct and mature.

It should answer:

* who Igal is
* what he builds
* which roles he fits
* what technologies he uses
* what the visitor should do next

Possible hero style:

* left side: headline, short summary, role tags, CTAs
* right side: compact recruiter snapshot or AI guide preview
* no large abstract 3D visuals
* no heavy background animation

Possible headline:

> Full-stack developer building SaaS products and AI-assisted workflows.

Possible supporting copy:

> I’m a software developer based in Leipzig, working with TypeScript, Vue/Nuxt, React/Next, Node.js, APIs, and practical AI automation. I help product teams turn ambiguous ideas into reliable, user-focused software.

## Navigation

Navigation should be simple:

```text
Igal Vilensky
Work
Experience
AI Guide
Contact
```

Optional:

```text
CV
GitHub
LinkedIn
```

Avoid too many nav items.

## Cards and Components

Cards should be clean and content-led.

Use:

* subtle borders
* soft shadows only if needed
* clear headings
* small role/tech tags
* concise descriptions
* outcome highlights
* obvious links

Avoid:

* glassmorphism-heavy surfaces
* excessive rounded corners
* loud shadows
* animated hover effects that distract
* too many icons

## Project Presentation

Projects should be presented as case studies, not just gallery cards.

Each featured project card should answer:

* What is it?
* Why is it relevant?
* What was Igal’s role?
* What stack was used?
* What outcome or proof exists?
* Why should a recruiter care?

Featured cards should be limited. Prefer fewer strong projects over many weak ones.

## AI Guide Visual Treatment

The AI guide should feel like a smart search layer over the portfolio.

It should not look like:

* a cartoon bot
* a mascot
* a fake human assistant
* a customer support widget
* a sci-fi terminal

It should look like:

* a professional assistant panel
* a recruiter shortcut
* a grounded Q&A interface
* a modern command/search component

Possible labels:

* Ask My Portfolio
* Portfolio Guide
* Recruiter Guide
* AI Portfolio Guide

Suggested UI:

* compact panel on homepage
* suggested prompt chips
* clear disclaimer
* concise answer area
* links to relevant projects/sections

Example suggested prompts:

```text
What roles is Igal a good fit for?
Summarize Igal for a recruiter.
Show me his strongest AI-related projects.
What is his frontend experience?
Does he have SaaS experience?
Which project should I review first?
```

The AI guide should use restrained visual signals. A small sparkle or assistant icon is acceptable, but avoid playful chatbot branding.

## Motion and Interaction

Use minimal motion:

Allowed:

* subtle hover states
* soft transitions
* small reveal animations
* smooth scrolling
* command-palette-like AI guide interaction

Avoid:

* large GSAP sequences
* constant marquee movement
* dramatic text reveals
* animated backgrounds
* motion that delays reading

Respect reduced-motion settings.

## Content Density

The site should be easy to read.

Use:

* short paragraphs
* bullet lists where helpful
* section summaries
* clear labels
* visual grouping
* enough whitespace

Avoid:

* long walls of text
* too many projects on the homepage
* dense skill clouds
* large unstructured timelines

## Imagery

Use images only where they add credibility or context.

Possible image types:

* profile photo, used professionally and calmly
* project screenshots
* architecture diagrams
* UI screenshots
* simple system diagrams

Avoid:

* decorative stock images
* abstract AI images
* random gradients
* excessive personal imagery

## Accessibility

The design must prioritize accessibility.

Requirements:

* strong color contrast
* keyboard-friendly navigation
* visible focus states
* semantic headings
* readable font sizes
* reduced motion support
* clear links and buttons
* no important information hidden only in hover states

## Mobile UX

Mobile must feel first-class.

Recruiters may open the portfolio from LinkedIn on mobile.

Mobile requirements:

* fast hero scan
* clear CTAs
* readable project cards
* no horizontal overflow
* no tiny tags
* no heavy animations
* easy contact access

## Design Anti-Goals

Do not make the portfolio:

* flashy
* overly artistic
* neon/cyberpunk
* animation-heavy
* generic AI-generated
* too playful
* visually noisy
* project-gallery-only
* CV pasted into a website
* too corporate and lifeless

## Design Success Criteria

The design is successful if:

* the first screen feels calm, confident, and professional
* the site is easy to scan
* the strongest projects stand out
* the AI guide feels useful and mature
* the visual style supports trust
* the portfolio feels aligned with full-stack, SaaS, and AI automation roles
* nothing feels gimmicky
* the user experience is better than the visual decoration
