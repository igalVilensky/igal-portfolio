# Portfolio Positioning

## Purpose

This portfolio is a recruiter-friendly developer portfolio for Igal Vilensky.

Its purpose is to help recruiters, hiring managers, technical leads, and potential collaborators quickly understand:

* who Igal is professionally
* which roles he is targeting
* what technical skills he brings
* what kind of products he has built
* how his current AI agents and automation upskilling connects to his existing software development experience
* why he is a strong candidate for full-stack, frontend, SaaS, and AI automation-related roles

The portfolio should not feel like a generic personal website, creative playground, or visual experiment. It should feel like a mature product-engineering portfolio.

## Core Positioning

Igal is a full-stack software developer with professional SaaS and web development experience, currently expanding his profile toward AI agents, automations, and practical AI-assisted product workflows.

He is not positioned as a beginner entering tech from zero.

He is positioned as:

> A full-stack software developer with real product experience, now specializing further in AI-assisted workflows and automation-ready web applications.

## Tone

The tone should be:

* clear
* confident
* grounded
* mature
* practical
* direct
* human
* product-minded
* recruiter-friendly

* hype
* buzzwords without proof
* exaggerated claims
* “AI guru” language
* “creative technologist” as the main identity

## AI Guide Positioning

The portfolio may include an AI guide feature called “Ask My Portfolio” or similar.

This feature should be positioned as:

> A grounded AI guide that helps recruiters quickly explore Igal’s experience, projects, role fit, and technical strengths.

It should not be a gimmick, mascot, or general chatbot.

It should demonstrate:

* AI product thinking
* grounded answers
* structured context
* safe limitations
* recruiter-focused UX
* practical AI integration

The AI guide must work as an optional enhancement. The portfolio must remain useful without it.

## AI Guide Boundaries

The AI guide should:

* answer only from provided portfolio/CV/project data
* say when information is not available
* guide users to relevant sections or projects
* support recruiter questions
* summarize Igal’s role fit
* explain project relevance
* provide concise answers by default

The AI guide should not:

* invent facts
* browse the web unless explicitly implemented later
* make hiring promises
* claim private or unavailable information
* send emails
* apply to jobs
* schedule meetings
* expose API keys
* present itself as Igal
* pretend to know more than the provided data

Preferred framing:

> Open to full-stack, frontend, SaaS, and AI automation-related software developer roles in Germany, Leipzig, hybrid, or remote EU contexts.

The site should communicate readiness, clarity, and direction.
